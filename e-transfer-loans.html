How Interac e-Transfer works

Why e-Transfer is the fastest way to receive your funds

If you've ever sent money to a friend using your bank's app, you already know how Interac e-Transfer works. We use the same technology to deliver your loan.

1
We send to your email or phone
Once your loan is approved and signed, we initiate an Interac e-Transfer to the email address or phone number linked to your bank account.
2
You get a notification instantly
Your bank or email notifies you that funds are waiting. Most Canadian banks auto-deposit e-Transfers so the money lands directly — no action needed from you.
3
Funds appear in your account
If your bank has Autodeposit enabled (most do by default), the money is in your account within minutes. If not, you accept the transfer in your banking app — takes seconds.
4
Use your money immediately
Funds are available right away — pay a bill, make a purchase, or withdraw cash. No hold periods, no delays, no waiting until morning.

Once you are approved and have e-signed your loan agreement, we initiate an Interac e-Transfer immediately. The transfer typically arrives in your bank account within approximately 2 minutes. If your bank has Autodeposit enabled — which most Canadian banks do by default — the funds land automatically with no action required from you. The entire process from starting the application to money in your account usually takes under 10 minutes.
Yes — our system runs 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year, including evenings, weekends, and holidays. Interac e-Transfer itself is a real-time payment rail that processes at all hours. There is no time of day or night when funding is unavailable. If you are approved at 3 AM on Christmas morning, you receive your funds at 3 AM on Christmas morning.
No — Autodeposit is not required, but it does make the process faster. If you have Autodeposit enabled through your bank's app, the funds land in your account automatically within minutes with nothing for you to do. If you don't have it set up, you'll receive an email notification and simply need to log into your banking app to accept the transfer — a process that takes about 30 seconds.
The e-Transfer is sent to the email address you provide during your application — which should be the same email registered with your bank for Interac e-Transfer. If your bank uses your phone number instead of an email for Autodeposit, please provide that in your application so your funds arrive without manual intervention.
