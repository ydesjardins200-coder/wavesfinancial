Signal Details
🚫 NSF Count (90 days)
Max 25 pts — highest weight signal
Non-sufficient funds events in the last 90 days sourced from Flinks bank statement analysis. Frequent NSFs indicate payment failure patterns and budgeting instability.
0 NSF → 0 pts 1–2 → 8 pts 3–5 → 16 pts 6+ → 25 pts
⛔ Payment Oppositions
Max 20 pts — hard gate at 3+
Pre-authorized debit reversals detected in the bank transaction feed. Even a single opposition signals a history of PAD failures, which is Waves' primary collection mechanism.
0 → 0 pts 1 → 10 pts 2 → 20 pts 3+ → Hard decline
📊 Debt-to-Income (DTI)
Max 20 pts
Ratio of (fixed obligations + estimated biweekly loan payment × 2) to verified monthly income. High DTI means the borrower's cash flow leaves little room for an additional payment obligation.
<30% → 0 pts 30–50% → 8 pts 50–70% → 14 pts 70%+ → 20 pts
📈 Income Regularity
Max 15 pts
Pattern of income deposits detected by Flinks. Consistent paycheque cadence signals stable employment. Irregular or undetected income makes PAD timing unpredictable.
consistent → 0 pts variable → 6 pts irregular → 12 pts undetected → 15 pts
⚖️ Income Mismatch
Max 10 pts
Compares declared monthly income (form) to Flinks-verified income. Large gaps suggest misrepresentation. Calculated as |declared − verified| / declared.
<15% gap → 0 pts 15–35% → 5 pts 35%+ → 10 pts
💰 Balance Cushion
Max 10 pts
Ratio of average daily balance to requested loan amount. A low balance relative to the loan means there may not be sufficient funds in the account when the first PAD processes.
balance >30% of loan → 0 pts >10% → 4 pts ≤10% → 10 pts
